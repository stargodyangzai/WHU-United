About

Benefiting from the rapid development and widespread application of the Unitree G1 platform, WHU United is developing a RoboCup 2026 adult humanoid robot system, integrating reinforcement learning-based low-level motion control with competition-adapted software stacks. We aim to implement advanced RL motion primitives on the G1, such as stable walking, autonomous standing recovery and kicking. Moreover, we adapt the modular RoboCup architectures of Booster-T1 and B-Human to the G1, providing core competition components like perception-action integration, motion interfaces and behavior execution. For better team performance, we also integrate multi-agent strategy modules from 3D simulation experience, including passing tactics and dynamic forward role switching based on game scenarios. These implementations are expected to underpin reliable on-field operation and support the subsequent development of RoboCup 2026.

Core Robot Specifications

Unitree G1 Comp is a competition-level humanoid robot launched by Unitree Robotics, specially built for event scenarios such as RoboCup, integrating technical achievements in reinforcement learning, bionic motion control, and real-time environmental perception.

Unitree G1 Comp Technical Specifications

Robot Height 1320mm
Robot Weight 35kg
Walking Speed 2m/s
Degrees of Freedom 31
Motor Type Torso Robot Integrated Joint (Max Torque 120 N·m)
Sensors Camera, Intel RealSense D455 (Infrared Disabled); Gyroscope, 6-axis High-Stability IMU
Control Unit High-Performance Real-Time Motion Control Unit
Perception & Decision Unit NVIDIA Jetson Orin 16GB (Edge Computing Platform)
Material Aluminum Alloy & High-Strength Engineering Plastic
Battery 13S Ternary Lithium Battery
